Learn More

In 2007, I graduated from Louisiana State University in elementary education. I began teaching at the age of 23 and now I have been a teacher for 14 years. I have remained at the same school during my career. I currently teach first grade, but I have taught grades 1, 2, and 3. I have served as grade level chair for ten years. Throughout my career, I have gained a range of expertise especially with teaching students that come from poverty. I have learned a lot about helping children develop the habits required to become lifelong inquirers. Many of my students come to school without their basic needs met. I always begin the year focusing on building trust with my students. Trust between a teacher and student is critical for student growth. I am a strong believer in positive classroom management. Praise is a key component to keeping motivation levels high with students. Many students come from homes full of stress, so it is essential to create a learning environment that fosters a sense of community, happiness, and growth mindsets. My students come to me with a wide range of ability levels. Differentiating learning is a critical component of my instruction. Differentiation is crucial in a class of diverse learners. Success with students occur when I make decisions based on their needs.
