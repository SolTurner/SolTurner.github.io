Reflection

London

My personal teaching philosophy has always been to create a learning culture that helps students develop understanding and respect of one another so that all participants learn from one another, gain a growth mindset, and to develop habits of a life-long learner. Throughout my career thus far, I have taken every advantage I have been given to grow and learn. The professional developments, learning opportunities, and degree advances have helped me model what I want my students to achieve as they get older.

As I began teaching at age 23, I never imagined that I would have stayed at one school in Baton Rouge, Louisiana throughout my career. I always dreamed of traveling the world and teaching at International schools during my career. One major reason for this is that I traveled a lot when I was little with my family. My father was an educator that traveled the world. I was born in India, moved to Japan, Brazil, Florida, Massachusetts, Tennessee, and finally landed in Louisiana. While I stayed in Louisiana for college, my family moved onto Amsterdam and now are located in the Philippians. When I traveled the world, I was exposed to many cultures that triggered my thought process. I have a gained a great appreciation and understanding of many cultures. There is something so exhilarating to me about learning how different cultures approach aspects of life that are different than my own. As my life did not allow me to follow through with my dream, I have found ways to stimulate my mind and grow my mindset right from my hometown.
