Specialist Degree Program

In the spring of 2020, I was accepted to the Learning Technologies and Design Educational Specialist degree program by the University of Missouri. My anticipated graduation date is Spring of 2022. The program requires 30 graduate credit hours. The program is designed to train students in the following areas: design learning environments; develop learning systems apps; understand usability; user experience; new technology and communication; improve workplace performance; learn best practices for needs assessment; task analysis, and course design and development. I chose this degree so that I could increase my knowledge of technology as it plays such a vital role in education. I hope to gain knowledge of the competencies so that I can broaden my expertise in education. The program has already taught me so many skills that I would not have even known how to begin learning on my own. Even the instruction in my classroom has improved as I have learned how to effectively analyze the needs of my students and implement best practices to improve student outcomes. I have widened my knowledge of technology platforms and how to use technology in the classroom to improve instruction.The overarching goals of the program are listed below. I have also included some artifacts that demonstrate my ability to meet the goals of the program.

Leadership Program

At the beginning of the 2020-2021 school year, I was accepted by the East Baton Rouge Parish school board to be part of the 2020-2021 Instructional Leader for Tomorrow Cohort IX. The program is for inspiring leaders. Although, I have not completed the program yet, my anticipated completion date is June 2021. I have included more details on the leadership page. The goal of the East Baton Rouge Leadership program is to develop a pool of qualified candidates to fill the need for new exceptional school leaders in the district. The program is set up like a college course through Canvas. There is a text aligned with the content of the program. I attend live classes 1-3 times a month and submit work on each topic for feedback. I am also assigned to an administrator that is not my current administrator as my mentor. I stay in communication with my mentor throughout the process and shadow her in action for 35 hours throughout the school year. The overall focus of the program is to train and support the candidates in the following areas: data driven instruction, observation and feedback, instructional planning, professional development, student culture, staff culture and managing school leadership teams. I am also working with my cohort to learn about EBR budgeting with both Title I and the General Funds, master scheduling, the role of the school counselor at all school levels, parental and community involvement, using the Teacher Evaluation rubric, writing a school improvement plan, mock interviews, and certification in non-violent crisis involvement. The compass leader rubric established by the Louisiana department of education is used to determine the readiness of the participants to lead. My involvement with the program has given me great insight into what is required of the leaders in my district. Meeting many of the important leaders in the district and attending the classes they have provided throughout the program has allowed me to gain knowledge from their expertise. One of the requirements of the program included completing the COMPASS Training for Evaulators. I have included my certificaton for proof of completion. I have also included a piece of my writing that explains what I think exceptional leadership is.

Mentor Program


In the spring of 2020, the Louisiana State Department decided that all mentor teachers must complete a mentoring program and take an assessment to prove they have the competencies to be a mentor. Although, I have not completed the program yet, my anticipated completion date is May of 2021. This program requires me to complete a self- paced learning course on mentoring new teachers through APEL. Once the modules are completed, a four-part assessment through BloomBoard is required for certification. The assessment process through BloomBoard is very time consuming and requires artifacts to be submitted by myself and my mentee for approval of passing. The program focuses on Mentoring for content instruction, mentoring for classroom management, building knowledge through content-rich text-sets, and adapting high-quality material to meet student needs. For each section, I had to analyze the needs of my mentee and students, develop a systematic plan, implement the plan and collect many artifacts to prove the plan was working, and evaluate the progress of my mentee and students. My mentee and I worked together to identify areas of growth that we felt would help her improve her in all of the areas listed above. The artifacts I have included on this page are just a few of the artifacts that I feel represent the work that has gone into completing the assessments to be a certified mentor. This experience has forced me to look deeply into my own teaching, coaching, and leadership skills and analyze them for improvement. Throughout the process, I kept a growth mindset, therefore, I have strengthened my habits in each area.
