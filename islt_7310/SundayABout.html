About Me

I hold a Bachelor's degree in Elementary Education (Grades 1-8) from LSU and a Master's degree in Educational Leadership from Southeastern University. I currently attend the University of Missouri to gain my specialist degree in Learning Technologies and Design (expected graduation date is Spring 2022). For the past fourteen years I have taught in the challenging but dynamic environment of Highland Elementary School located in East Baton Rouge Parish in Louisiana. I have taught grades one, two and three and have served as grade level leader. During the past thirteen years, I have successfully mentored field experience students through LSU’s College of Education. Over the last five years, I have successfully mentored Holmes students from the College of Education. Over the course of my career, I have learned much about helping children develop the habits required to become lifelong inquirers. I have had the good fortune to have completed training in the IB PYP model up through Level 2. I have also been able to visit a leading PYP school in Europe on several occasions and have completed a powerful workshop on Cultures of Thinking with Ron Ritchhart of Harvard's Project Zero. I was also selected to attend a symposium in Princeton for Women in Leadership. I am currently enrolled in APEL’s mentor teacher program and plan on being certified by the summer of 2021. This year, I was also accepted into the East Baton Rouge Parish Leadership program. My completion date is June of 2021.
