Leadership Cohort


The goal of the East Baton Rouge Leadership program is to develop a pool of qualified candidates to fill the need for new exceptional school leaders in the district. The program is set up like a college course through Canvas. There is a text aligned with the content of the program. I attend live classes 1-3 times a month and submit work on each topic for feedback. I am also assigned to an administrator that is not my current administrator as my mentor. I stay in communication with my mentor throughout the process and shadow her in action for 35 hours throughout the school year. The overall focus of the program is to train and support the candidates in the following areas: data driven instruction, observation and feedback, instructional planning, professional development, student culture, staff culture and managing school leadership teams. I am also working with my cohort to learn about EBR budgeting with both Title I and the General Funds, master scheduling, the role of the school counselor at all school levels, parental and community involvement, using the Teacher Evaluation rubric, writing a school improvement plan, mock interviews, and certification in non-violent crisis involvement. The compass leader rubric established by the Louisiana department of education is used to determine the readiness of the participants to lead. My involvement with the program has given me great insight into what is required of the leaders in my district. Meeting many of the important leaders in the district and attending the classes they have provided throughout the program has allowed me to gain knowledge from their expertise. One of the requirements of the program included completing the COMPASS Training for Evaulators. I have included my certificaton for proof of completion. I have also included a piece of my writing that explains what I think exceptional leadership is.
